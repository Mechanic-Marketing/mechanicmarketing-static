The Google Business Profile Checklist Every Workshop Should Run Quarterly

12 Sep 2025 · 5 min read

If you had to pick one free marketing asset that punches hardest for a local workshop, it's your Google Business Profile, not your website. It's what shows up in the map pack, it's what people check before they call, and most workshops set it up once and never touch it again.

  • Categories, hours, and services need checking quarterly, not just at setup.
  • Photos go stale fast, and stale photos read as an inactive business.
  • Q&A and review responses are searchable and count toward relevance, not just trust.
  • Posts (offers, updates, tips) are one of the most underused features on the platform.

Categories and Services

Your primary category should match your core business exactly, "Auto Repair Shop" rather than something vaguer. Secondary categories should reflect your actual specialities, European, diesel, 4x4, whatever applies. Add or remove services in the Services section any time your offering changes.

Photos That Are Actually Recent

A profile with photos from three years ago signals a business that isn't being actively managed. Add new photos of the team, the workshop, and completed jobs every few months, it's a small task that quietly builds trust.

Reviews and Q&A

Respond to every review, good or bad, within a few days. Google's own algorithm treats profile activity as a relevance signal, and customers reading reviews notice when an owner replies thoughtfully to a negative one.

Use Posts, Don't Ignore Them

Google Business Profile Posts let you share offers, updates, or tips directly in your listing. Most workshops never touch this feature, which means the ones that do stand out with almost no effort.

Common Questions

How often should I actually check this?

Quarterly is a good minimum, monthly if you have the time. Photos and posts can go up more often than that.

Does responding to bad reviews really help?

Yes, a thoughtful reply to a negative review often reassures future customers more than a page of five-star reviews with no engagement.

Do I need a professional photographer?

No, clear, well-lit phone photos of your real workshop are fine, and often feel more authentic than polished stock shots.

What's the fastest win if I've never touched my profile?

Check your categories and services first, that's the part most likely to be outdated or incomplete.
