5 Auto Glass Repair Marketing Tips to Book More Jobs Fast

8 Mar 2025 · 4 min read

Auto glass repair shops face a common problem — standing out in a crowded market. You might offer the best service in town, but if no one knows about it, you're missing out on paying customers. Here are five strategies that actually work.

#1 — Get Your Google Business Profile Sorted

When someone searches "windshield repair near me," they won't scroll forever. A complete and optimised Google Business Profile helps customers find you on Google Maps and search results. Make sure your business name, address, phone number, hours, and services are consistent everywhere. Post before-and-after repair shots, photos of your shop, and happy customers. Ask every satisfied customer for a detailed review. Use Google Posts to share deals, seasonal offers, and updates — it keeps your business looking active and trusted.

#2 — Run Google Ads That Bring in Real Jobs

Paid ads work fast when set up correctly. Use local keywords like "auto glass repair in [your city]" so your ads reach people nearby. Add call assets so customers can ring you straight away — if they have to search for your number, they'll call someone else. Run remarketing ads to follow up with visitors who didn't book. Track conversions carefully: if an ad isn't bringing in calls or bookings, adjust it. Don't burn cash on clicks that don't turn into jobs.

#3 — Make It Easy to Get Insurance Claims Sorted

Many auto glass repair jobs go through insurance. If you make the claims process easy — handling the paperwork, liaising directly with the insurer, and offering zero-excess repairs where applicable — you remove the biggest friction point for customers. Promote this on your website and in your ads: "We handle the insurance claim for you."

#4 — Partner with Fleet Operators and Strata Managers

Fleet vehicles, delivery vans, and company cars regularly need glass repairs. A contract with a local fleet operator or strata management company can provide consistent, recurring work. Visit local businesses directly, offer a trade rate, and make it as easy as possible for them to send vehicles your way.

#5 — Offer Mobile Service at High-Traffic Locations

If you offer mobile repairs, promote the convenience aggressively. "We come to you — home, work, or roadside" is a significant differentiator over shops that require customers to drive in. Partner with car parks, shopping centres, or workplaces to offer on-site repairs. The convenience factor alone can command a premium and generate strong word-of-mouth.
