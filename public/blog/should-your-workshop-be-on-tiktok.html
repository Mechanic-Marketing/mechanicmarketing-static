Should Your Workshop Be on TikTok?

16 Jan 2026 · 5 min read

Every year there's a new platform workshops feel pressure to jump on, and right now it's TikTok. Before spending time on it, it's worth asking honestly whether it fits how your customers actually find a workshop, rather than assuming it's mandatory because everyone's talking about it.

  • TikTok works best for workshops with a genuinely visual or entertaining angle, restorations, unusual repairs, satisfying before/afters.
  • It's a discovery platform, not a search platform, people aren't searching "mechanic near me" on TikTok.
  • Building an audience takes real time investment, it's not a quick win like Search ads.
  • For most single-location workshops, it's a nice-to-have, not a priority over Search and Google Business Profile.

What TikTok Is Actually Good At

TikTok rewards visually interesting, short-form content, an engine teardown, a satisfying repair, a before-and-after transformation. If your workshop naturally produces that kind of content, it can build brand awareness and local recognition over time.

What It's Not Good At

It's not a lead-generation engine in the way Search Ads or a well-optimised Google Business Profile is. Someone with a broken-down car today isn't opening TikTok to find a mechanic, they're searching Google or asking around. TikTok builds awareness for later, not bookings for today.

The Real Question: Do You Have the Content?

TikTok success depends on consistent, genuinely interesting content, not polished ads. If nobody on your team is excited to film and post regularly, it's going to fizzle the same way most workshop Facebook pages do. Be honest about whether that appetite exists before investing time.

Where It Fits in the Bigger Picture

If your fundamentals, Search Ads, Google Business Profile, a converting website, are solid, TikTok can be a worthwhile addition for brand building. If those fundamentals aren't sorted yet, TikTok is a distraction from the channels that will actually fill bays this month.

Common Questions

Is TikTok worth paid ads, or just organic?

For most workshops, organic first, paid ads on TikTok make more sense once you already have some content and audience traction.

How much time does it realistically take?

Enough that it needs to be someone's actual task, not an afterthought. A few hours a month for planning and filming is a realistic minimum.

What if we're not a "flashy" workshop, is there still an angle?

Yes, straightforward diagnostic content, common problems explained simply, or genuine before/afters can still perform well without needing to be flashy.

Should we do TikTok instead of Facebook?

Not necessarily instead, they serve different audiences and habits. Prioritise based on where your actual customers spend time.
