The 2026 Workshop Growth Report: Why We're Doubling Down on Search

13 Oct 2026 · 6 min read

Every year the marketing landscape shifts, and every year workshops get pulled toward whatever's newest. Looking back across 2026 so far, the pattern for our best-performing clients has been consistent, and it isn't a new platform. It's a renewed focus on Search, done properly.

  • Search intent remains the strongest predictor of a booking, across every industry we work in.
  • Google Business Profile optimisation is delivering some of the best ROI of any channel this year.
  • Content built around specific services and suburbs continues to outperform broad, generic pages.
  • Newer platforms have their place, but they're supplementing Search, not replacing it.

What We're Seeing Across Client Accounts

Workshops that invested consistently in Search, both paid and organic, through the first half of 2026 are showing steadier lead flow and lower cost per booking than those chasing newer, trendier channels. It's not glamorous, but it's working.

Google Business Profile Is Punching Above Its Weight

Of all the channels we manage, Google Business Profile optimisation, reviews, posts, accurate categories, has consistently delivered outsized results relative to the time it takes. For local trades, it remains one of the highest-leverage, lowest-cost improvements available.

Specific Beats Broad, Again and Again

The workshops seeing the strongest growth aren't the ones with the biggest budgets, they're the ones with the most specific content. Dedicated pages for individual services and suburbs are consistently outperforming generic "our services" pages in both rankings and conversion rate.

Where We're Cautiously Testing New Channels

We're not ignoring newer platforms, but we're testing them as a layer on top of a solid Search foundation, not as a replacement for it. Budget goes to Search first, proven and predictable, before it goes to anything experimental.

Common Questions

Does this mean Facebook and other social platforms aren't worth it?

Not at all, they still have a role, particularly for brand awareness and retargeting. They're just not the primary lead driver Search is for most workshops.

What should I prioritise if my budget is limited?

Google Business Profile optimisation first, it's often the highest-ROI, lowest-cost improvement available. Search Ads and dedicated service pages come next.

Is this trend likely to hold for the rest of 2026?

Based on what we're seeing across client accounts, yes, Search intent remains the strongest signal of someone ready to book, and that's unlikely to change quickly.

How do I know if my workshop is set up to take advantage of this?

Check whether you have dedicated pages for your key services and suburbs, and whether your Google Business Profile is fully optimised. If not, that's the starting point.
