Likes Don't Pay the Rent: Why We're Ignoring 'Engagement' to Focus on Bookings

1 Sep 2026 · 4 min read

A social post with hundreds of likes feels good. It also doesn't pay the rent, cover wages, or book a single car in. If your social media strategy is judged by likes, comments, and shares instead of calls and bookings, you're optimising for the wrong thing.

  • Engagement metrics are easy to measure and easy to feel good about, which is exactly the problem.
  • A viral post can bring zero bookings, a boring one can bring five.
  • Bookings, calls, and form fills are the only metrics that actually matter for revenue.
  • Vanity metrics are worth tracking only as a secondary signal, never the goal.

Why Engagement Became the Default Metric

Platforms show you likes and comments front and centre because it keeps you posting, not because it's what drives your business forward. It's the easiest number to see, so it's become the easiest number to chase, even when it has nothing to do with your bottom line.

What We Actually Track Instead

For every dollar spent on social, whether organic effort or paid ads, we're asking one question, did it lead to a call, a form submission, or a booking. Everything else is secondary. A post that gets ten likes but drives two bookings beats a viral post that drives none, every time.

The Danger of Chasing Vanity Numbers

Content built purely to be shareable often drifts away from your actual services and your actual customer. Funny videos and trending audio can build a following, but if that following isn't converting into local customers who need a mechanic, it's not doing its job.

What to Measure Instead

Track clicks to your booking page, phone calls attributed to social, and form submissions. If a platform can't show you that connection clearly, it's worth asking whether it deserves your time and budget.

Common Questions

Should I stop posting content that doesn't perform well engagement-wise?

Not necessarily, judge it by bookings and enquiries, not likes. Some of the best-converting content gets modest engagement.

Is paid social still worth it for workshops?

Yes, when it's tracked properly and tied to a clear call to action, not just brand awareness.

How do I track bookings from social if I don't have a big marketing budget?

Simple tracking links and a dedicated phone number or booking URL for social traffic go a long way, even without expensive tools.

What's a realistic booking goal from social media?

It varies by workshop, but the goal should be a specific, trackable number, not "more engagement".
