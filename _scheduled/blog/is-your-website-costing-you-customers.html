Is Your Website Costing You Customers? (The 'Ick' Factor)

6 Oct 2026 · 5 min read

There's a specific feeling a visitor gets on an outdated, clunky website, a small, immediate sense that something's off. It's rarely conscious, but it's real, and it's costing workshops customers before a single word of copy is even read.

  • Design and trust are more connected than most workshop owners realise.
  • An outdated site quietly signals an outdated business, whether that's fair or not.
  • Slow load times and broken layouts on mobile amplify the "ick" factor significantly.
  • Fixing the feel of a site is often cheaper than people assume, and pays for itself quickly.

What Causes the "Ick" Factor

Tiny, hard-to-tap buttons on mobile. Fonts that don't match. Stock photos that clearly aren't your workshop. Contact details that are hard to find. None of these are catastrophic on their own, but stacked together, they create a subconscious sense that the business behind the site might not be as sharp as it claims.

Why This Matters More for Trades Than People Think

Customers already carry some wariness about mechanics, worried about being overcharged or sold something they don't need. A dated, clunky website reinforces that wariness instead of easing it. A clean, modern, trustworthy-feeling site does the opposite, it starts the relationship on the right foot before the first phone call.

The Mobile Test

Pull up your own website on your phone right now. Does it load fast? Can you tap the phone number without zooming in? Is it obvious what to do next? If any of those feel clunky to you, on a device you know how to use, imagine the experience for a stranger.

This Doesn't Require a Full Rebuild

Often it's a handful of targeted fixes, updated photography, cleaner mobile navigation, a modern colour scheme, that shift a site from "off" to "trustworthy", without the cost or downtime of building an entirely new site from scratch.

Common Questions

How do I know if my site actually has this problem?

Ask a few customers, or better, a few people who've never used your workshop, to look at it and give you their honest first impression.

Is this just about looks, or does it affect SEO too?

Both. Slow load times and poor mobile experience directly hurt search rankings, not just first impressions.

What's the fastest win if I can only fix one thing?

Mobile speed and navigation. That's where most visitors will land first, and where clunkiness is felt most sharply.

Do I need professional photos, or will phone photos do?

Good phone photos of your real workshop and team beat generic stock photos every time. Authenticity matters more than production value here.
