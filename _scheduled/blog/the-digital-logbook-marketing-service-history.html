The Digital Logbook: Why Your Marketing Needs a Service History

25 Aug 2026 · 5 min read

A car with no service history is a risk, you don't know what's been done, what's overdue, or what's about to break. Most workshops run their marketing the exact same way, no ongoing record, just a vague sense of "we're doing Google Ads" and hoping it's fine.

  • A marketing service history tracks what's been changed, tested, and why, over time.
  • Without it, every new person touching your marketing starts from zero.
  • Documented changes make it possible to tell what actually caused a result, up or down.
  • A logbook approach turns marketing from guesswork into a maintainable system.

What a Marketing Logbook Actually Looks Like

It doesn't need to be complicated. A simple running record, what changed, when, and why, for your ads, your website, and your Google Business Profile is enough to turn scattered activity into something you can actually manage and improve.

Why This Gets Skipped

Workshops are busy, and marketing often feels like a "set it and check the phone" task. But without a record, you can't tell whether last month's dip was the new competitor down the road, a seasonal slowdown, or a change you made to your ad copy three weeks ago.

The Cost of Not Having One

Every time a workshop switches agencies, staff, or tools without a documented history, they're starting the diagnosis from scratch. That's wasted time and, often, wasted budget spent re-testing things that were already tried and already failed.

Start Small

Even a basic spreadsheet logging major changes, budget shifts, and results by month is enough to start. The goal isn't perfect documentation, it's having enough history to make informed decisions instead of guesses.

Common Questions

Isn't this just reporting?

Reporting shows results. A service history also shows the changes behind those results, which is what makes future decisions faster and better.

Who should be responsible for keeping it up to date?

Whoever manages your marketing day to day, whether that's you, a staff member, or your agency. The key is that it's someone's job, not an afterthought.

Does this apply to organic and paid marketing, or just ads?

Both. Website changes, Google Business Profile edits, and content updates are just as worth tracking as ad account changes.

How far back should the history go?

As far back as you have records for. If you're starting from nothing, that's fine, just start today.
