Search vs. PMax: Why Workshops Are Returning to Traditional Search Ads in 2026

21 Jul 2026 · 5 min read

Performance Max was sold as the future, set a budget, let Google's algorithm handle the rest. For a lot of workshops, that "set and forget" promise has turned into "set and wonder where the budget went". In 2026 we're seeing more shops move budget back into traditional Search campaigns, and for good reason.

  • PMax bundles Search, Display, YouTube, and Discover into one black box, you can't see what's actually driving bookings.
  • Search campaigns give you full control over keywords, negative keywords, and ad copy.
  • Local, high-intent searches like "brake repair [suburb]" convert better when you can bid and write copy specifically for them.
  • PMax works best with a mature, high-volume account, most single-location workshops don't have that volume.

The Problem With Black Box Campaigns

PMax reporting groups everything into asset groups, not keywords. That means you can spend weeks running ads without knowing whether "logbook service" or a random Display placement on someone's blog is bringing in your leads. For a workshop watching every dollar, that lack of visibility is a real cost.

Search Intent Still Wins for Local Trades

Someone searching "mechanic near me open now" has already decided they need a workshop, today. That's the exact kind of high-intent, bottom-of-funnel search that Search campaigns are built to capture, with tight geographic targeting and negative keywords to keep out tyre-kickers.

Where PMax Still Makes Sense

It's not that PMax is bad, it's that it's built for a different job. Multi-location chains with large budgets and enough historical conversion data can get value from PMax's automation. A single-bay workshop spending a few thousand a month usually can't feed the algorithm enough data to make it worthwhile.

What We're Doing Differently in 2026

For most of our workshop clients, we're running tightly targeted Search campaigns as the core, with PMax used only as a supplementary layer once there's enough conversion history to make it useful. Control first, automation second.

Common Questions

Should I turn off PMax completely?

Not necessarily, it depends on your budget and how much conversion data you already have. For most single-location shops, Search should be the priority.

Is Search more expensive than PMax?

Not inherently, cost per click depends on competition and quality score, not the campaign type. The difference is you know exactly where the spend is going.

How do I know which one is working for my workshop right now?

Look at your campaign-level conversion data. If you can't see it broken down by keyword, that's the first sign it's worth a second look.

Can I run both at the same time?

Yes, plenty of workshops run both, the key is making sure PMax isn't cannibalising Search traffic that would have converted anyway.
